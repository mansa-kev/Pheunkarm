Course Overview

Our accredited Advanced Trading Course has been designed to enable you to build professional market expertise in just 12 weeks. Whether you wish to trade commodities, currencies, stock indices, or digital assets, we cover how these financial instruments are correlated and how to trade them safely.

The program combines structural academic modules with daily practical charting sessions inside our virtual trading environment. Observing price-action models in real-time allows candidates to apply technical tools, manage sizing limits, and understand trading psychology in simulated market situations.

Key Learning Objectives

  • Technical & Fundamental Mastery: Develop a comprehensive mapping of how macroeconomic calendars and candle analysis are combined by institutional traders.
  • Systematic Trade Execution: Learn to define realistic price targets and stop loss points based on price structures rather than hope.
  • Drawdown & Capital Control: Master sizing models and risk metrics to preserve your trading balance during drawdown cycles.
  • Behavioral Psychology: Identify hope, greed, fear, and over-confidence triggers, building a disciplined trading mindset.

Course Syllabus

Discover the step-by-step curriculum designed to take you from market fundamentals to trading strategy execution.

  • Understanding major asset classes: Forex, Equities, Commodities, and Crypto.
  • Currency pairs, base/quote concepts, and contract sizes (lots).
  • Introduction to charting platforms: Order tickets, bid-ask spreads, and limit orders.
  • Basics of candlesticks and chart layouts.
  • Drawing major price levels: Support, resistance, trends, and channels.
  • Japanese candlestick pattern analysis to identify sentiment shifts.
  • Moving Averages and MACD: Identifying underlying trends and crossover signals.
  • Bollinger Bands, Average True Range (ATR), and momentum oscillators (RSI & Stochastics).
  • How political cycles and macroeconomic events cause large market movements.
  • Reading interest rate decisions, inflation data (CPI), and employment reports (NFP).
  • Building a daily macroeconomic calendar checking checklist.
  • Analyzing central bank statements and hawkish/dovish indicators.
  • Position sizing mathematical models (Percent Risk Model).
  • Drawdown limits, daily stop bounds, and portfolio diversification rules.
  • Managing emotions when holding winning or losing trades.
  • Keeping a detailed trading log and analysis diary to spot cognitive biases.
  • Replicating specific trade setups: Straddles, pivot point retests, and Holy Grail layouts.
  • Setting realistic price targets and risk-to-reward ratios (minimum 1:2).
  • Backtesting strategies with historical data to calculate win rates.
  • Writing your personalized trading business plan.
