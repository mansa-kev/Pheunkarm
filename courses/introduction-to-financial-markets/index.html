Introduction to Financial Markets

Take your first step in trading. Explore the structure of global markets, study price charts, and execute your first simulator orders in a 1-week focused syllabus.

View Curriculum Ask an Advisor

Course Overview

This focused one-week trading course is designed specifically for beginners who have no prior knowledge of financial analysis or asset management. We work from the ground up, starting with simple market structures and defining vocabulary terms clearly.

Through live streaming sessions, you'll learn the daily habits of retail traders: tracking major calendars, drawing support and resistance lines, and interpreting key candlestick signals. Students are given simulator trading accounts to test their ideas safely with the guiding hand of our mentoring desk.

Key Learning Objectives

  • Market Jargon & Structure: Demystify the financial arena. Understand the roles of retail brokers, central banks, and market makers.
  • Introduction to Charting: Learn to read candle components, identifying trends, support zones, and basic momentum signs.
  • Economic Calendar Basics: Find out which reports (like inflation releases or rate changes) spark market moves.
  • Risk Preservation: Master basic stop-loss practices to protect your capital.

Course Syllabus

Explore the five introductory steps of the financial market syllabus.

  • What are financial markets, who trades them, and why retail platforms are growing.
  • Understanding terminology: bullish/bearish, bids, offers, margins, and leverage.
  • Roles of central banks, commercial brokers, and investment desks.
  • Introduction to macroeconomic indicators: Interest rates, CPI, GDP, and labor.
  • Central banks and monetary policies.
  • Government fiscal policies and tax/spending impacts.
  • Reading raw charts: Identifying support lines and resistance bands.
  • Introduction to Japanese candlestick charts and core candle signals.
  • Basic trend analysis using simple moving averages.
  • The absolute rule: Placing stop losses to avoid large account losses.
  • Understanding natural cognitive biases (hope, greed, loss aversion).
  • How to manage basic risk allocations per trade.
  • Hands-on virtual practical sessions on our demo platform.
  • Applying candlestick charts and support/resistance lines to real-time asset moves.
  • Reviewing trades and questions with our advising coaches.
